I saw kamelot play in the O2 forum kentish town with support from ad infinitum , blackbriar and frozen crown The show started early at with frozen crown making thunderous entrance with there 3 guitarists and constant double bass but still managed to have epic riffs and lots of melody

After them was blackbriar who unfortunately got sick so the drummer asked the audience to be patient and they'd give it their best but they still gave a great performance especially their bass player, who was jumping and running all around the stage and providing a lot of energy to the performance. They opened with Mortal Remains, where the singer came out in a black veil providing a gothic flair to their music their music was very atmospheric Next was ad infinium who have a very modern metal sound they had a very minimalistic set-up compared to the other bands recently the band have incorporated more electronics into there sound they opened with Follow Me Down where Melissa Bonnyâ€™s cleans perfectly matched her harsh vocals which I found the most impressive out of ad infinium set

After this was kamelot the headliners, it has been 5 years since kamelot were last in the UK and they brought back all the stops for their triumphant return with smoke,confetti and pyro sparklers all with an impressive backdrop. They started their ninety minute set with Veil of elysium, where the band entered one by one, then started the song. Both Thomas Youngblood and Sean Tibbetts where constantly running around the stage which kept the energy high and they had also had added a platform at the front of the stage to get closer to the audience which made the audience feel included and at points i was only 2-3 metres away from the band during the song new babylon the stage lifted up and their singer above the stage as he sung the chorus. In this song Melissa Bonny came back and provided extra vocals to a few of the songs before wrapping up with a drum and keyboard solos. After they came back for an encore with one more flag in the ground and liar liar which had the whole crowd singing along
