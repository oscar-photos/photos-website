I saw Leap play in the Dingwalls in Camden with support from cortney dixon The show started with the band playing an acoustic version of Downfall outside the venue right before the show

After the performance the sold-out crowd went inside to the inmate venue and as support was Cortney dixon who played a short but lively set ,that got the whole crowd singing along , combining rock and pop with 80s disco aesthetics Headlining was leap playing a home show to finish of their downfall tour,They started with a bang opening with
"The downfall" showing the command the band had over the audience.They continued with hit after hit as the mosh-pit continued with more and more ferocity and the temperature rose and rose.

About halfway through their set they paused to get the crowd to sing happy birthday to their bass player ,Declan Brown, and during the next song they got the whole mosh-pit to do a ballerina dance. after another song they played the at the time unerased song called "sinking feeling" before slowing it down for "burnt matches" and allowing the crowd to cool off in the very hot venue The set concluded With the hits "One Million pieces" and "exit signs" as one final hurrah to send off the tour. after a few minutes of Chants for one more song they came out for a short encore where the mosh-pit reached its climax as they played a few of their hits before saying farewell to the crowd for one final time.
